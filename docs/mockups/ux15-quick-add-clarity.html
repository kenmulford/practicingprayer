UX-15: Quick Add Clarity

Three concepts for helping users understand where Quick Add prayers go

Concept A — Inline Helper Text

A small muted paragraph below the title field explains the destination and how to reorganize later.

Pros: Zero extra UI; always visible; no interaction needed.
Cons: Static text may be ignored over time; no way to change destination.
Best for: Minimal change, purely informational.

Concept B — Header + Card Picker

A warm subtitle sets context, and an optional card picker (defaulting to Quick Add) lets users choose a destination right away.

Pros: Solves the clarity problem AND lets power users skip reorganizing later; Quick Add becomes a universal "add prayer" entry point.
Cons: Adds UI weight; picker may confuse new users who don't have cards yet; more implementation work.
Best for: Turning Quick Add into a flexible shortcut.

Concept C — Contextual Tip Banner

A warm-toned coaching banner at the top of the form explains the behavior. Dismissible so it doesn't clutter repeat visits.

Pros: Friendly and non-intrusive; disappears once understood; warm tone fits the journal aesthetic.
Cons: Once dismissed, new info is gone (unless it reappears); slight vertical space cost.
Best for: Onboarding-style guidance that fades away.
